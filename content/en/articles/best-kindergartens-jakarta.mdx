Choosing a kindergarten is one of the first big decisions expat families make after landing in Jakarta. The city has an impressive range of English-language and international early-years options — from prestigious through-schools that run from kindergarten to Grade 12, to warm, dedicated preschools focused purely on the early years.

This guide rounds up **10 of the best kindergartens in Jakarta for expat families**, with each school's location, curriculum, age range, and an indicative fee so you can shortlist quickly. If you'd like the bigger picture on how early-years education works here first, see our companion [guide to preschools in Jakarta](/en/articles/preschools-jakarta-expat-guide).

<Callout type="warning">
  **About the fees:** the figures below are indicative annual early-years tuition for the 2026 school year, gathered from published fee schedules. They **exclude** many one-time and recurring extras — registration, admission/development fees, deposits, uniforms, transport, and lunch — which can add tens (or hundreds) of millions of rupiah. Fees change yearly and vary by age group and campus, so always confirm the current, itemised schedule directly with the school.
</Callout>

## 1. Jakarta Intercultural School (JIS)

The oldest and most prestigious international school in the city, **JIS** runs an inquiry-based international programme (IB continuum) across campuses in Pondok Indah, Cilandak, and Pantai Indah Kapuk. Its Early Childhood division starts from age 3. Expect the highest fees in Jakarta: early-childhood tuition starts around **Rp 130 million/year**, but mandatory annual capital fees and one-off charges push the real first-year cost well above Rp 200 million. Best for families wanting a blue-chip, globally recognised school with deep resources.

## 2. British School Jakarta (BSJ)

Set on a large green campus in **Bintaro**, **BSJ** follows the British **Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS)** and welcomes families from more than 50 nationalities. It takes children from age 2 (Nursery) through to Year 13. Early-years tuition starts around **Rp 150 million/year** and rises through the kindergarten years, with a one-time application fee and refundable enrolment deposit on top. A strong choice for British-curriculum families.

## 3. Australian Independent School (AIS) Jakarta

**AIS** offers the Australian curriculum alongside the IB, with early-learning campuses in **Pejaten and Kemang** — right in the heart of expat South Jakarta. It accepts children from around 2–3 years. Early-years costs sit at roughly **Rp 100–165 million/year** (the first year is higher because of one-off fees). A good middle option for families wanting an established international school without the very top-tier price tag.

## 4. Nord Anglia School Jakarta (NAS)

Part of the global Nord Anglia family, **NAS Jakarta** (in **Cilandak**) follows the English National Curriculum and takes its youngest learners from **18 months** in a purpose-built early-years area with soft-play and messy-play zones. First-year enrolment for a two-year-old is around **Rp 120 million**, plus a non-refundable application fee. Appealing for families who value the Nord Anglia global network and early starting age.

## 5. New Zealand School Jakarta (NZSJ)

Tucked into leafy **Kemang**, **NZSJ** follows New Zealand's **Te Whāriki** early-years framework — a warm, play-based approach centred on the whole child — and accepts children from 18 months. It's one of the more accessible international options at this age: a half-day pre-school place starts around **Rp 58 million/year**. Great for families who want a nurturing, play-first environment.

## 6. North Jakarta Intercultural School (NJIS)

For families in **North Jakarta**, **NJIS** in **Kelapa Gading** runs a hands-on preschool programme for ages 2–4 with small classes (around 10–15 students) and internationally qualified teachers. Fees are provided on request. A convenient, well-regarded international option away from the South Jakarta cluster.

## 7. Springfield School

**Springfield** operates modern campuses in **Permata Buana (West Jakarta)** and **Cibubur (East Jakarta)**, making it handy for families outside the usual south-side hubs. It's an international through-school for ages 2 to 18, with an early-years programme that blends US, UK, and Singaporean resources. Fees are on request. Worth a look if you're based in the west or east of the city.

## 8. Kinderland

A long-running **Singapore-based** preschool brand, **Kinderland** has several Jakarta campuses (including Lebak Bulus and Muara Karang) and takes children from 18 months to 6 years. Fees run roughly **Rp 40–80 million/year** — a big step down from the full international schools — making it a popular, more affordable bilingual-friendly choice.

## 9. Global Sevilla

**Global Sevilla** runs preschool and kindergarten at its **Puri Indah (West)** and **Pulo Mas (East)** campuses, using the **EYFS** curriculum with added mindfulness activities. It's among the most budget-friendly on this list: toddler tuition from around **Rp 25 million/year** rising to about **Rp 45 million/year** for kindergarten, plus a one-off development fee. A solid value pick for west/east Jakarta families.

## 10. Tutor Time

An established international preschool and kindergarten brand operating since 1996, **Tutor Time** has its flagship in **Pondok Indah** plus branches in North and West Jakarta, serving ages 1–6 in an English-language, play-based environment. Fees vary by centre and by how many sessions a week your child attends, and sit well below full international-school tuition — confirm the current schedule with your nearest campus.

## How to Choose

With this much choice, a few questions help narrow the field quickly:

* **Location and commute** — Jakarta traffic is real; a school close to home matters enormously for a young child.
* **Curriculum and continuity** — if you may relocate again, a widely recognised curriculum (British, IB, Australian) travels well.
* **Age and start date** — some schools take babies from 18 months; others start at 3.
* **Total cost** — look past headline tuition to registration, capital/development fees, deposits, and extras.
* **Language environment** — fully English, or bilingual with Bahasa Indonesia?
* **Visit in person** — nothing beats seeing a normal school day and how staff interact with the children.

## FAQs — Kindergartens in Jakarta

<FAQ question="How much does kindergarten cost in Jakarta?">
  It varies enormously. Top international schools' early-years tuition runs from around Rp 120–200 million per year or more, before one-off fees. Dedicated preschools and more affordable kindergartens range from roughly Rp 25–80 million per year. Always confirm current fees and extras directly with the school.
</FAQ>

<FAQ question="What age is kindergarten in Jakarta?">
  Kindergarten (locally "Taman Kanak-Kanak" or TK) generally covers ages 4–6, but many international schools accept children into their early-years programmes much earlier — some from 18 months, others from age 3.
</FAQ>

<FAQ question="Which is the most expensive kindergarten in Jakarta?">
  Jakarta Intercultural School (JIS) is typically the most expensive, with early-childhood costs well above Rp 200 million per year once mandatory capital and one-off fees are included. British School Jakarta and the other established international schools are also in the premium tier.
</FAQ>

<FAQ question="Are there affordable international kindergartens in Jakarta?">
  Yes. Dedicated preschools such as Global Sevilla (from around Rp 25 million/year) and Kinderland (around Rp 40–80 million/year) are far more accessible than the full international schools, while still offering English-language or bilingual, internationally influenced programmes.
</FAQ>

<FAQ question="Where do most expat families send their children to kindergarten in Jakarta?">
  Most international kindergartens cluster in South Jakarta — Kemang, Cilandak, Pondok Indah, and Pejaten — with strong options also in North (Kelapa Gading), West (Puri Indah, Permata Buana), and satellite areas like Bintaro.
</FAQ>
