**Brighton Montessori River Valley** is a SPARK-certified private preschool along River Valley Road, serving families in central Singapore from infant care through kindergarten. As one of several centres under the Brighton Montessori group — which has operated in Singapore for nearly three decades — the River Valley campus is known for its spacious layout, Montessori-led teaching, and convenient access for working parents.

## Overview

Located at **370 River Valley Road**, the centre sits in a quieter, leafy part of the River Valley district. It is within walking distance of **Fort Canning MRT** and well served by nearby bus routes, making drop-offs and pick-ups manageable for parents commuting from the CBD or surrounding neighbourhoods.

Brighton Montessori River Valley accepts children from **2 months (infant care)** through **Kindergarten 2 (6 years old)**, covering playgroup, nursery, and kindergarten levels. The school operates **Monday to Friday, 7:00am to 7:00pm**, which suits dual-income households that need extended hours.

As a **private, non-anchor operator** centre, fees sit in the mid-to-upper range for Singapore preschools. The school is **SPARK-certified**, signalling that it meets Singapore's quality standards for preschool education. Chinese is offered as the **mother tongue** language, with optional afternoon enrichment programmes such as the Intensive Chinese Immersion Programme (ICIP) and Kidz Fun Discovery Programme (KFDP).

Parents generally describe the centre as calm, well-organised, and focused on individual pacing — hallmarks of authentic Montessori practice rather than a heavily academic, drill-based approach.

## Curriculum

Brighton Montessori follows a **Montessori-based curriculum** built around Maria Montessori's philosophy: children learn at their own pace through hands-on, concrete-to-abstract activities in a prepared environment.

The programme is structured around **five Montessori pillars**:

- **Practical Life** — daily living skills, independence, and fine motor development
- **Sensorial** — activities that refine the senses and build cognitive foundations
- **Language** — phonics, reading readiness, and communication through one-to-one and small-group work
- **Mathematics** — manipulatives and sequential materials that introduce numeracy concepts
- **Cultural Studies** — geography, science, and world awareness woven into everyday learning

Beyond core Montessori work cycles, children are exposed to broader subjects to support a well-rounded transition to primary school. Teachers emphasise **individualised instruction** rather than whole-class lecturing, which suits children who benefit from moving ahead in some areas while taking more time in others.

Afternoon **enrichment programmes** (availability and timing may vary by centre) include Mandarin immersion, art and craft, cookery, music and movement, speech and drama, and multimedia activities. These are designed to extend literacy, social-emotional skills, and creative expression beyond the morning Montessori block.

## Facilities

The River Valley campus is frequently praised for its **spacious and inviting environment**. Classrooms are well equipped with Montessori materials and arranged to encourage free exploration within clear boundaries — a balance that supports both independence and safety.

Key facility highlights include:

- **Generous indoor space** compared with many city-centre preschools, giving children room to move during work cycles and group activities
- **Prepared Montessori classrooms** with age-appropriate learning materials across all levels
- **Green, tranquil surroundings** that contribute to a calmer daily atmosphere
- **Extended operating hours** (7:00am–7:00pm) with structured routines from infant care through kindergarten

The centre has undergone **recent renovations**, which many parents note has refreshed classrooms and common areas. As with any upgrade period, it is worth confirming the current layout and any ongoing works during a school tour.

Security, cleanliness, and daily routines are managed with the structured approach typical of established preschool chains, though individual experiences can vary by class and lead teacher.

## Fees

Brighton Montessori River Valley is a **private operator**, so fees are set by the school and are **not capped** at AOP/POP rates. Figures below are **monthly school fees before ECDA subsidies and GST**, based on publicly filed rates — always confirm the latest amounts directly with the centre.

| Programme | Approx. Monthly Fee (before subsidy) |
| --- | --- |
| Full-day infant care | $2,700 |
| Half-day infant care | $1,500 |
| Full-day childcare (playgroup–K2) | $2,320 |
| Half-day childcare (playgroup–K2) | $1,830 |

**Additional costs** to budget for may include:

- **Registration fee** (one-time)
- **Deposit** (typically one month's fee)
- **GST** (currently 9% on fees)
- **Uniforms, materials, and enrichment** (if selected)

Singapore Citizen families may qualify for **ECDA childcare subsidies** (Basic and Additional), which can reduce out-of-pocket costs meaningfully depending on household income and work status. PR and foreigner families should enquire directly about applicable rates, as subsidy eligibility differs.

For a personalised fee breakdown, contact the centre at **6588 3883** or book a tour through the [official website](https://www.brightonmontessori.com.sg/river-valley/).

## Pros and Cons

**Pros**

- Authentic **Montessori approach** with individualised pacing and hands-on materials
- **SPARK-certified** centre with a long-established operator track record
- **Spacious campus** in a central yet quieter River Valley location
- **Full-day and half-day options** from infant care through K2
- **Extended hours** (7:00am–7:00pm) ideal for working parents
- **Good transport access** near Fort Canning MRT and major bus routes
- **Strong parent communication** and structured transition support toward Primary 1

**Cons**

- **Premium pricing** compared with anchor/partner operator centres
- **Not an AOP/POP centre**, so fee caps and subsidy structures differ from government-linked preschools
- **Demand can be high** in central locations — waitlists are possible for popular levels
- **Montessori style may not suit every child** — less structured, whole-class instruction than some academic-focused preschools
- **Enrichment and mother tongue programmes** may add to total monthly cost

## Final Verdict

Brighton Montessori River Valley is a **strong choice for parents who value authentic Montessori education** in a central Singapore location and are willing to pay private-sector fees for smaller-group, individualised learning.

The centre stands out for its **space, calm environment, and consistent Montessori methodology** backed by an operator with decades of experience. It is particularly well suited to families who want extended hours, a smooth infant-to-kindergarten pathway on one campus, and a preschool that prioritises independence and love of learning over early academic pressure.

Before enrolling, **visit the centre in person**, observe a class in session, and ask about class sizes, teacher turnover, and current fee packages including subsidies. Every child responds differently to Montessori environments — a trial visit will tell you more than any review.

**Recommended for:** Working parents in central Singapore seeking a SPARK-certified Montessori preschool with full-day care from infancy through kindergarten.

**Less ideal for:** Budget-conscious families prioritising lowest fees, or parents who prefer a highly structured, exam-oriented preschool model.
